The Department of Computer Science (DCS) at the University of Ghana (UG) is a leading institution in computer science education and research in Ghana. Founded in 1963, the department has been instrumental in shaping the Ghanaian IT landscape by producing highly skilled computer science professionals who have made significant contributions to the nation's development.

Faculty and Staff

  • Professors: Distinguished scholars with significant experience and expertise in various areas of computer science. They often engage in research, mentorship, and leadership roles within the department.
  • Associate Professors: Faculty members who have demonstrated a high level of proficiency in teaching and research. They may also take on administrative responsibilities.
  • Administrative Personnel: This includes individuals responsible for the day-to-day administrative tasks, such as departmental secretaries, administrative assistants, and other support staff.
  • Staff might include administrative personnel, technical support, and possibly graduate assistants.
  • Graduate Assistants: These are often graduate students who assist faculty with teaching, research, and administrative tasks. They gain valuable experience while supporting the department's activities.
  • Lecturers: These individuals focus primarily on teaching. They might have practical industry experience and contribute to the department's educational initiatives.

Academic Programs

The department offers a comprehensive range of undergraduate and graduate programs in computer science:

    Undergraduate Programs

  • Bachelor of Science in Computer Science (BSCS) Single-Major (3:2:1:1)
  • Bachelor of Science in Computer Science (BSCS) Major-Minor (3:2:2:1)
  • Bachelor of Science in Information Technology (BSIT)

    • Graduate Programs:

  • Master of Philosophy (MPhil) in Computer Science
  • Master of Science (MSc) in Computer Science
  • PhD in Computer Science

Research

The department is actively engaged in cutting-edge research in a variety of areas, including:
  • Artificial intelligence
  • Machine learning
  • Natural language processing
  • Computer vision
  • Software engineering
  • Network science
  • Information security

Impact

The department's graduates are highly sought-after by employers in Ghana and around the world. They are employed in a variety of sectors, including:
  • Information and communication technology (ICT)
  • Banking and finance
  • Telecommunications
  • Manufacturing
  • Education
The department also plays a key role in promoting ICT adoption and development in Ghana. It collaborates with government agencies, non-governmental organizations, and private sector companies to develop and implement ICT solutions that address national challenges.

In recognition of its excellence, the Department of Computer Science has been ranked among the top five computer science departments in Africa by the Times Higher Education (THE) Africa University Rankings.

The department is proud of its contributions to the development of Ghana's ICT sector and looks forward to continuing to play a leading role in shaping the future of technology in the country.
